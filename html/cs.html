I admit I use (and write code for) UEFI (2.8 and earlier), and I also admit that I am not using/coding Legacy BIOS anymore, but I have a reason... a strong reason! This does NEVER mean I support Secure Boot. You people need to stop judging me, and use your brain for a minute please!

8086 was a 1970s thing. I should nott be writing bunch of useless real-mode and protected-mode code in order to boot up my long-mode kernel or code any simple or advanced system-level application. BIOS needs a lot of unnecessary assumptions. By 2020, BIOS has officially become a pain in the... neck.

Some cool titles for learning Computer Science foundations

As many people ask me for book/reference recommendations, here are some textbooks that I used to use back at undergraduare school (between 2011 and 2016):

  • Computer Programming:
    • The C Language: What is better than ANSI C Standard Specification?
    • Assembler - x86 family: I used to use Barry Brey as a start. It also covers x86 computer design.
    • Assembler - i386: Intel i386 standard specification has mostly everything you need.
    • Assembler - x86_64: Although it looks ugly, amd64 manual was not too bad for me.
    • Assembler - ARM: Cortex A Programmer's Guide for beginners, ARMv8 Architecture Reference for everything.

  • Computer Engineering Courses:
    • Digital Logic: Floyd is great for first timers, Digital Logic Design for advanced courses.
    • Computer Engineering Fundamentals: My professor used Clements' Principles of Computer Hardware. This book is really great.
    • Computer Organization: My professor used Patterson's Computer Organization and Design (MIPS).
    • Computer Architecture: My professor used Patterson's Qunatitative Computer Architecture.
    • Embedded Systems: My professor used Mazidi's AVR and Embedded Systems book.
    • Microprocessor Systems: Mazidi's IBM PC and Compatible Computers will let you love your PC.
    • Operating Systems: My OS professor used Tanenbaum and Stallings. I added the handsome OS 3/e.
    • Computer Networks: Tanenbaum's Computer Networks and CCNA Material.

  • Electrical Engineering Courses:
    • Modern Physics: Serway's Physics for Scientists and Engineers book.
    • Electric Circuit Analysis: Sadiku's Fundamentals of Electric Circuits for a high GPA!
    • Transistor Citcuit Analysis: Sedra teaches you everything from BJT/MOSFET to RAM circuits.
    • Electromagnetics: Theraja covers everything your professor might think of.
    • VLSI IC Design: CMOS VLSI Design by Weste and Harris.
    • Automatic Control Systems: Old is Gold (Linear), Phillips (Nonlinear/Digital), and Dorf (Modern Theory).
    • Digital Signal Processing: Signal Processing for Communications.
    • Communications Theory: Modern Communications Systems (covers both Analog and Digital).

  • Computer Science:
    • Switching Circuit Theory: Switching and Finite Automata Theory and the original thesis by Shannon.
    • Data Structures: The Art of Computer Programming by genius Donald Knuth.
    • Mathematical Foundations of Computer Science: Discrete Mathematics by Susanna.
    • Theory of Computation (Automata/Computability): Elements of Theory of Computation and Alan Turing's Original Paper.
    • Theory of Computation (Computational Complexity): Introduction to Algoithms by Cormen et al.
    • Formal Language Theory (and Compilers): The Dragon Book.
    • Information Theory: David MacKay and Shannon's Original Paper.
    • Artifical Intelligence Theory: Artificial Intelligence - A Modern Approach by Russel and Norvig.
